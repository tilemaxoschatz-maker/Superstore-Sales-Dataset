# Superstore Sales Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data from a Superstore business to identify key revenue drivers, customer behavior patterns, regional performance, and sales trends.

The goal is to provide actionable business insights that can support decision-making and improve overall business performance.

---

## Business Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze sales performance across products, customers, categories, regions, and shipping methods.

The analysis aims to identify the key drivers of revenue, discover high-performing and underperforming business areas, and provide data-driven recommendations that can help improve sales performance and business growth.

---

## Dataset Information

- Records: 9,800
- Features: 18
- Source: Superstore Sales Dataset

---

## Data Cleaning

The following data cleaning steps were performed:

- Checked for missing values
- Removed rows containing missing Postal Code values
- Checked duplicate records
- Converted date columns to datetime format
- Created a new business feature: Shipping_Days

---

## Business Questions

1. What is the total sales generated?
2. Which product categories generate the highest sales?
3. Which sub-categories generate the highest sales?
4. Which regions generate the highest revenue?
5. Which states generate the highest sales?
6. Which customers generate the most revenue?
7. Which shipping mode is used most frequently?
8. Which shipping mode generates the highest sales?
9. What are the monthly sales trends?
10. What are the yearly sales trends?
11. Which customer segments generate the most sales?
12. What is the average order value?
13. Is there a relationship between shipping days and sales?

---

## Key Findings

- Technology generated the highest sales among categories.
- Phones and Chairs were the strongest-performing products.
- California was the highest-revenue state.
- The Consumer segment generated the largest share of sales.
- November was the strongest sales month.
- Sales increased significantly from 2015 to 2018.
- Shipping speed showed almost no relationship with sales value.
